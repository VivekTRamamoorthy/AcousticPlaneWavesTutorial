Basics

What is sound?

Sound as we hear it, is simply the fluctuations in air pressure over and above the atmospheric pressure, which is ~ 100 000 Pascals, repeating between 20 and 20,000 times a second.

\(P = P_{atm} +p\)

where \(P_{atm}\) is the atmospheric pressure, \(p\) is the acoustic pressure, and \(P\) is the total pressure.

When we speak, our vocal chords vibrate and produce pressure disturbances that oscillate several hundreds of times in a second. These oscillations propagate at the speed of sound. Our ears detect these pressure changes and our brains process it as language.
